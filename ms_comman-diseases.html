1. Common Cold

Causes: Caused by rhinoviruses and other respiratory viruses. Spread through airborne droplets or direct contact. Often worsens in colder months or crowded spaces.

Symptoms: Sneezing, runny nose, sore throat, mild cough. Low fever, fatigue, and watery eyes may occur. Usually lasts 5–7 days and is self-limiting.

Aid:Rest, drink plenty of fluids, use OTC meds. Warm soups, throat lozenges, and steam inhalation help. No antibiotics needed as it’s viral.

2. Influenza (Flu)

Causes: Caused by influenza viruses (types A and B).

Symptoms: Fever, chills, body aches, fatigue, and cough.

Aid: Rest, fluids, and antiviral medications if prescribed.

3. Food Poisoning

Causes: Consuming contaminated food or beverages.

Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps.

Aid: Hydration and rest; medical attention if severe.

4. Diarrhea

Causes: Often caused by infections, food intolerances, or medications.

Symptoms: Frequent loose or watery stools, abdominal pain.

Aid: Rehydration, electrolyte solutions, and dietary changes.

5. Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Causes: Viral or bacterial infections, allergens, or irritants.

Symptoms: Redness, itching, and discharge from the eye.

Aid: Cool compresses and eye drops; antibiotics for bacterial infections.

6. Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

Causes: Bacterial infection, often from E. coli.

Symptoms: Frequent urination, burning sensation, and cloudy urine.

Aid: Increased fluid intake and antibiotics as prescribed.

7. Strep Throat

Causes: Caused by Streptococcus bacteria.

Symptoms: Sore throat, difficulty swallowing, and fever.

Aid: Antibiotics and pain relievers for symptom management.

8. Sinusitis

Causes: Infections, allergies, or nasal polyps causing sinus inflammation.

Symptoms: Nasal congestion, facial pain, and thick nasal discharge.

Aid: Decongestants, nasal sprays, and steam inhalation.

9. Skin Rashes (Allergic Dermatitis)

Causes: Allergic reactions to substances like soaps, plants, or metals.

Symptoms: Red, itchy patches on the skin.

Aid: Avoiding irritants, antihistamines, and topical steroids.

10. Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu)

Causes: Often viral infections, but can also be bacterial or parasitic.

Symptoms: Diarrhea, vomiting, stomach cramps, and fever.

Aid: Hydration, rest, and over-the-counter medications to relieve symptoms; seek medical attention if severe.

11. Bronchitis (Acute)

Causes: Typically caused by viral infections or irritants like smoke.

Symptoms: Coughing, mucus production, fatigue, and shortness of breath.

Aid: Rest, hydration, and inhalers for symptom relief.

Symptoms: Sore throat, swollen tonsils, and difficulty swallowing.

16. Sprains and Strains

Causes: Injuries to ligaments (sprains) or muscles/tendons (strains).

17. Hemorrhoids

Causes: Swollen blood vessels in the rectal area due to straining.

Symptoms: Pain, itching, and bleeding during bowel movements.

Aid: Dietary changes, sitz baths, and over-the-counter creams.

18. Migraine

Causes: Various triggers including stress, hormonal changes, and certain foods.

Symptoms: Severe headache, nausea, and sensitivity to light/sound.

Aid: Rest, hydration, and medications to relieve pain.
