BLEEDING

Bleeding refers to the loss of blood from the circulatory system.

It can be classified into three main types:
1. Arterial Bleeding: Bright red blood that spurts with each heartbeat.
2. Venous Bleeding: Dark red blood that flows steadily from a wound.
3. Capillary Bleeding: Slow, oozing blood from minor cuts or scrapes.

First Aid for Bleeding:

- First, call emergency services.
- If an object is embedded in the wound, don't remove it.
- Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth around the object if any.
- Elevate the injured area above the heart if possible.
- If bleeding doesn’t stop, apply more pressure and seek medical help.
- Monitor the person for shock (pale skin, weakness, rapid pulse).
- Keep the person calm and still.

BURNS

Burns are damage to skin or deeper tissues caused by sun, hot liquids, fire, or chemicals.

Types of Burns:
1. First-Degree Burns: Affect only the outer layer of skin (epidermis).
2. Second-Degree Burns: Affect both the outer layer and the underlying layer (dermis).
3. Third-Degree Burns: Extend into deeper tissues, affecting all layers of skin and possibly underlying fat.

First Aid for Burns:

- Cool the burn with cool water (10-20 minutes).
- Cover with a clean, non-stick bandage.
- Avoid using creams or butter.
- Seek medical help for severe burns or if the burn is larger than the person’s hand.
- Do not pop blisters if present.
- Keep the area clean to prevent infection.

SPRAINS AND STRAINS

Sprains and strains involve stretching or tearing of ligaments and muscles, respectively.

First Aid for Sprains and Strains:

- Use the RICE method (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation).
- Apply ice for 15-20 minutes every 2 hours.
- Compress the area with an elastic bandage.
- Seek medical advice if severe swelling or pain persists.
- Avoid putting weight on the injured area.
- Monitor for signs of severe injury (increased pain, numbness).

FRACTURES

Fractures are breaks or cracks in bones.

First Aid for Fractures:

- Immobilize the injured area.
- Avoid moving the broken bone.
- Apply a splint if trained to do so.
- Seek emergency medical help immediately.
- If the bone is protruding, cover the area with a sterile dressing.
- Monitor for shock and keep the person calm.

NOSEBLEEDS

Nosebleeds are common and occur when the blood vessels in the nose break.

First Aid for Nosebleeds:

- Sit upright and lean forward.
- Pinch the nose for 5-10 minutes.
- Avoid tilting the head back.
- Seek help if bleeding doesn’t stop after 20 minutes.
- Apply ice to the nose to constrict blood vessels.
- Keep calm to avoid increasing blood pressure.

CHOKING

Choking occurs when an object blocks the airway, preventing normal breathing.

First Aid for Choking:

- Encourage coughing if the person can breathe.
- Perform the Heimlich maneuver if the airway is blocked.
- Call emergency services if the person becomes unconscious.
- If trained, give back blows and abdominal thrusts.
- After dislodging, monitor the person for breathing difficulties.

ELECTRIC SHOCK

Electric shock occurs when a person comes into contact with an electric current, potentially causing injury or death.

First Aid for Electric Shock:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Do not touch the person until the source of electricity is turned off.
- If safe, move the person away from the electrical source using a non-conductive object.
- Check for responsiveness and breathing.
- If unconscious and not breathing, start CPR.
- Monitor for burns and other injuries.

POISONING

Poisoning occurs when a person ingests, inhales, or comes into contact with harmful substances.

First Aid for Poisoning:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by poison control.
- If the person is conscious, ask them what they ingested.
- If contact poison is on the skin, rinse with water for 15-20 minutes.
- Keep the person calm and still.

INSECTS BITES AND STINGS

Insect bites and stings can cause allergic reactions, pain, and infection.

First Aid for Insects Bites and Stings:

- Remove the stinger if present (scrape it out).
- Wash the area with soap and water.
- Apply a cool compress to reduce swelling.
- Use over-the-counter pain relievers if necessary.
- Monitor for allergic reactions (difficulty breathing, swelling).

SEIZURES

Seizures are sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbances in the brain that can cause convulsions.

First Aid for Seizures:

- Stay calm and ensure the person is in a safe area.
- Move any objects away that could cause injury.
- Do not restrain the person or put anything in their mouth.
- Time the seizure; if it lasts longer than 5 minutes, call emergency services.
- After the seizure, place the person on their side to help with breathing.

HEART ATTACK

A heart attack occurs when the blood flow to a part of the heart is blocked, causing damage to the heart muscle.

First Aid for Heart Attack:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Have the person sit down and keep calm.
- If the person is conscious, give them aspirin (if not allergic).
- Loosen tight clothing and provide fresh air.
- Monitor their condition until help arrives.
- Be prepared to perform CPR if they become unresponsive.

STROKE

A stroke occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted, leading to brain damage.

First Aid for Stroke:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Note the time the symptoms started.
- Use the FAST method:
- F: Face drooping
- A: Arm weakness
- S: Speech difficulties
- T: Time to call emergency services.
- Keep the person comfortable and calm.
- Do not give them anything to eat or drink.

HEAT STROKES

Heat stroke occurs when the body overheats, often due to prolonged exposure to or physical exertion in high temperatures.

First Aid for Heat Strokes:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Move the person to a cooler environment.
- Remove excess clothing and apply cool water to their skin.
- Use fans or air conditioning to cool them down.
- If conscious, provide cool fluids to drink.
- Monitor their condition and be prepared to perform CPR if necessary.

DROWNING

Drowning occurs when a person is unable to breathe due to submersion in water or another liquid.

First Aid for Drowning:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Remove the person from the water as quickly and safely as possible.
- Check for responsiveness and breathing.
- If not breathing, start CPR immediately.
- If breathing, place the person on their side in the recovery position.
- Monitor their condition until help arrives.

ANAPHYLAXIS

Anaphylaxis is a severe allergic reaction that can be life-threatening.

First Aid for Anaphylaxis:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- If the person has an epinephrine auto-injector, help them use it.
- Keep the person lying down and calm.
- Monitor their breathing and pulse.
- Be prepared to perform CPR if they become unresponsive.

HYPOTHERMIA

Hypothermia occurs when the body loses heat faster than it can produce it, causing a dangerously low body temperature.

First Aid for Hypothermia:

- Call emergency services immediately.
- Move the person to a warm, dry place.
- Remove any wet clothing and wrap them in warm blankets.
- Provide warm, non-alcoholic beverages if conscious.
- Monitor their breathing and pulse.
- Be prepared to perform CPR if necessary.
